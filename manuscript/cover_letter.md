Dear Editor-in-Chief,  

We are pleased to submit our manuscript entitled **"Ten Years of GNSS-Denied Localization: A Systematic Literature Review on Sensor Modalities, Estimation Algorithms, and Evaluation Environments"** for consideration as a review article in *IEEE Transactions on Robotics*.

This systematic literature review covers a total of **995 peer-reviewed publications** from 2015 to 2026, establishing a comprehensive taxonomy of the field across five distinct dimensions: sensor modalities, estimation algorithms, application domains, evaluation environments, and benchmark datasets. 

The core contributions of this study are:
1. **Taxonomic Mapping:** We map the evolution and usage frequencies of 18 sensor modalities and 20 algorithm/framework categories, finding that visual-inertial combinations (Camera + IMU) and Visual-Inertial Odometry (VIO) have emerged as the dominant paradigms.
2. **Quantitative Accuracy Synthesis:** Through a systematic meta-analysis of quantitative accuracy (RMSE/ATE) reported across the literature, we provide empirical evidence of the accuracy distributions achieved by different sensor-algorithm classes.
3. **Cross-Tabulation and Trend Analysis:** We analyze sensor-algorithm co-occurrence, temporal trends showing the rise of Deep Learning-based estimators, and sensor usage patterns across different application domains (UAVs, UGVs, Spacecraft, etc.).
4. **Research Gaps & Future Directions:** We identify critical open challenges, such as the gap between simulation-heavy testing and real-world robustness, and provide actionable future directions for researchers.

This manuscript is original work and has not been published elsewhere, nor is it currently under consideration by another journal. All authors have read and approved the manuscript for submission. We declare no conflicts of interest.
